Introduction

This proposal is about the sitemap and wireframe of an application -- Presento. I started up Presento at the beginning of this year with a few friends, focusing on using AI technology to bring a difference to people's communication. This program has been stuck for a while since none of the team members are experts in UI/UX design, thus I see this as a great opportunity to progress and make Presento more complete.

Sitemap

I am planning to build my UX design portfolio with the following sitemap architecture. The design is instinctive to the users. Presento is an app, where we would like to display our product in the first place. After that, users will care about the price of our products. Thus we set the "Pricing division" right after the "Product" Page. Inspired by the Anaconda website, which is also a product-based app, there is a "Blog" division that updates the latest news of their product. Since Presento might involve more algorithm stuff, the "Blogs" can be some technical sharings contributed by the Presento Community. Last but not least is the "Teams" page, where you can see the contribution of each team member, and most importantly, find a way to join us!

Presento Sitemap

Wireframes

The landing page of Presento will contain some of the most important information, such as Product, Pricing, Blogs, Team, etc. Each division links to a page to expand the content itself. The landing page will be quite simple. It contains a slogan of our app, a one-sentence description of our mission, and a get started button.

As we can see in the navigation bar on the home page, you can access "Get Started" easily, which will show you clearer guidance of what you can do on our home page.

Then on each block in the "Get Started" page, it links to a division that expands itself. You have four options to get started, including Product, Blogs, Pricings and Team.
